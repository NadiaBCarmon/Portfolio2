Bio

The arts was one of my first loves growing up.

As a young girl I sold a drawing created in oil pastels and based on a Viking helmet at a local showcase in upstate New York.

I was an avid reader and music lover; indulging in everything from the tales of the witches and vampires from Anne Rice, the Edda Prose (Icelandic sagas), to collecting CDs and casettes from record stores or Heavy Metal labels like Century Media Records.

Fast forward 10+ years and my love of art and culture is as vital as ever.

As a Contributing Writer, i've written about film and music for 7 independent publications from 2010 to 2016; including the alternative lifestyle magazines Symbols, Prysm and DeVour, Chain DLK, The Offering: Heavy Metal Magazine, Graveyard Shift Sister, Black Girl Nerds, and most recently my company Nadia Carmon Inc. I've also written about marketing for the online resource the Digital Branding Institute.

But it was my stints as a Social Media Marketer and Event Planner for film producer and nightclub owner Noel Ashman, and as a Marketing and Advertising Intern for Prysm Magazine that made me realize my passion for promoting independent artists and brands.

This led me to create my freelance indie art and tech branding company Nadia Carmon Inc in the fall of 2016.

Now, after spending the last 2 years learning web development (Javascript, React and Ruby on Rails) in 5 courses at 3 coding bootcamps, I am using my skillset in frontend development, visual branding and content marketing to help brands tell their stories.
