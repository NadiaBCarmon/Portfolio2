Bio

The arts was one of my first loves growing up.

As a young girl I sold a drawing created in oil pastels and based on a Viking helmet at a local showcase in upstate New York.

I was an avid reader - indulging in everything from the tales of witches and vampires from Anne Rice to the Edda Prose (Icelandic sagas).

I was also an avid music lover and started collecting CDs and casettes around this time. Music catalogs, record stores, and Heavy Metal labels like Century Media Records were my havens for new sounds.

Fast forward 10+ years and my love of art and culture has continued to burgeon.

As an undergraduate Biology major with an Italian minor, I focused on Population Genetics. And while working in an anthropology lab focused on East African diversity, I was writing about film and music for 7 independent publications from 2010 to 2016; including Prysm and DeVour Magazine.

But it was my stints as a Social Media Marketer and Event Planner for film producer and nightclub owner Noel Ashman, and as a Marketing and Advertising Intern for Prysm Magazine that made me realize my passion for promoting independent artists and brands.

This led me to create my freelance indie art and tech branding company, Wet Ink Press Co (now Nadia Carmon Inc.)- in the fall of 2016.

Now, after spending the last 2 years learning web development (Javascript, React and Ruby) in 5 courses at 3 coding bootcamps, - including the New York Code + Design Academy, Fullstack Academy and the Bronx-based tech accelerator The Knowledge House, I am using my skillset in frontend development, visual branding and content marketing to help brands tell their stories.

Fun Facts



I am a screenwriter with 2 produced shorts to my name. Stranger and Coma are both psychological dramas starring Lydia Darly (Exorcist: The Beginning, The Way You Love), and directed by Jeremiah Kipp (Atlas of the Soul, The Minions, The Days God Slept). In 2014, Stranger was an Official Selection of The Online Film Festival (TOFF) and the Beyond the Beaten Path/Socal Independent Film Fest (Now the Huntington Beach Film Festival). While in 2015, it was an Official Selection of the Roxbury International Film Festival.

As a storyteller, I enjoy almost every genre. But Horror, Sci Fi, Crime Dramas and Action, sprinkled with copious amounts of humor are my favorites.

Currently i'm watching Hannibal, Hulu's The Path & Castle Rock, USA's The Sinner, and Supernatural. I will be embarking upon the challenge of writing my first feature-length screenplay in November.
